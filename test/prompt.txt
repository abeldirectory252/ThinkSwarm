Simulate a policy debate and strategic analysis around Ethiopia's digital payment competition between Telebirr (Ethio Telecom) and M-Pesa (Safaricom Ethiopia).

OBJECTIVE:
Model how different stakeholders react to a proposed National Bank of Ethiopia mandate requiring full interoperability between all mobile money platforms by Q1 2026. Predict the market outcomes, adoption patterns, and strategic responses over 12 months.

AGENT ROLES:
1. Ethio Telecom Executive — Defends Telebirr's market position. Argues against forced interoperability since it erodes their first-mover advantage. Proposes alternative: voluntary API partnerships with banks.
2. Safaricom Ethiopia CEO — Advocates strongly for interoperability. Argues it levels the playing field and benefits consumers. Presents M-Pesa Kenya case study as evidence.
3. NBE Governor — Must balance financial inclusion, monetary stability, and fair competition. Weighs input from all parties before issuing a regulatory decision.
4. Commercial Bank Association Rep — Represents CBE, Dashen, Awash Bank interests. Worried about deposit flight to mobile wallets. Pushes for mobile money operators to be regulated like banks.
5. Rural Merchant (Informal Economy Voice) — Represents the 15M+ informal sector workers. Cares about simplicity, agent availability, and low fees. Doesn't care about corporate politics — just wants to send and receive money easily.

SIMULATION RULES:
- Each agent presents their position in Round 1.
- In Round 2, agents respond to each other's arguments with counter-proposals.
- In Round 3, the NBE Governor synthesizes all input and announces a preliminary regulatory decision.
- In Round 4, all agents react to the decision and outline their 12-month strategic plan.
- Each agent must stay in character and argue from their stakeholder's genuine economic interests.

OUTPUT FORMAT:
1. Round-by-round debate transcript
2. Final regulatory decision summary
3. 12-month market impact forecast table (columns: Stakeholder, Predicted Action, Market Share Impact, Risk Level)
4. Key takeaways and policy recommendations
